Ventilating your home

Although air conditioning units have made our lives so much easier, they aren‚Äôt the most sustainable method of cooling a room. Air conditioners emit greenhouse gases, which contribute to global warming.

Conserving power

asdf

You may not realise how much power your devices are using while idle. Think about how many times you get up from your desk on a daily basis. You may get up to use the bathroom, make a sandwich, run some errands, or stretch your legs. When working, keep your devices on charge only when required.

Turn off or leave your devices on standby when you leave your desk. It will also pay to switch off any lights when they are not in use as well as unplugging electronics from the wall at the end of each workday. All these little acts to save power add up quickly.

This might sound like a lot of work, but it only feels that way at the beginning. Start building the habit by leaving sticky notes around, or hanging a piece of wall art that reminds you to do these things.

Within a couple weeks, these kinds of habits will be second-nature.

Reducing, reusing, recycling

asdf

As always, the three Rs are a great way to become more sustainable in practice.

Reduce the amount of waste produced. Minimise printing. Cook your lunch instead of buying takeaways to minimise plastic packaging. Install a filtered water tap in your home instead of buying plastic bottles.

Reuse or upcycle what you can. Print double-sided, buy second-hand office furniture and use a reusable, thermal flask for your coffee.

For recycling, allocate a dedicated bin to make it super easy.
